01 — The Product

What moves through
the chain?

Stepguard is a sensor box fixed underneath an escalator. Industrial IDS cameras continuously scan every step under low-light conditions. An on-edge AI vision model flags wear, damage and misalignment long before a breakdown — feeding a web dashboard that triggers a four-level alarm and schedules the fix.

The Sensor Box

A slim, patented unit that combines IDS industrial cameras, edge compute, and LED lighting into a housing mounted below the step band.

  • Width600–750 mm
  • Depth40–150 mm
  • Min. step distance100–200 mm
  • Max. step distance~500 mm
  • CameraIDS U3-3686XLE
  • Lifetime extension2× – 5×

MECHANISM
Cameras observe · edge AI classifies wear · cloud aggregates · dashboard alarms · technician dispatched.
02 — The Chain

From factory floor
to escalator step.

The supply chain splits into four phases. Hardware is sourced and built, Cboost integrates the AI stack on top of Levarne's platform, the system is installed and monitored in the field, and local partners handle repair. Every arrow is a handoff.

PHASE 01
Hardware
& Components
Cameras and sensor assembly are sourced and manufactured upstream of Cboost.
IDS Imaging
Supplier
German industrial-camera maker. Supplies the U3-3686XLE: a compact low-light camera that fits the tight, dark space beneath an escalator step.
Ex Robotics
Manufacturer
Assembles and distributes the Stepguard sensor hardware to Cboost. The factory endpoint where cameras, lighting and housing come together as a finished unit.
PHASE 02
Platform
& Capital
The hardware meets software, supported by the capital that makes expansion possible.
Cboost
CORE
Owns the patent-pending AI vision and predictive-analytics combination. Integrates hardware, trains the models, installs at customer sites, and runs the monitoring. Eighteen FTE, Den Dolder HQ.
Levarne
Software
Dutch software house behind the Eventual asset-management platform. Provides the dashboards, predictive alerts and customer-facing web interface.
VDB Vochtwering
Investor
Investment partner in Stepguard BV. Provides the external capital that — alongside Cboost's patent-tech equity and the DHI subsidy — funds German market entry, CE documentation and the first commercial pilot.
PHASE 03
Field Install
& Service
Cboost installs and monitors. The OEMs that own the escalator handle the physical repairs it flags.
Escalator OEMs
Repairs
The global escalator manufacturers — Schindler, KONE, TK Elevator, Otis — that already hold the maintenance contracts on most European installations. Stepguard plugs into their service workflow: when the AI flags wear, the OEM's technicians execute the fix.
Watertech
Sister platform
Shares Cboost's backend. Not a direct Stepguard supplier, but a bundling partner — one contract can cover water sensors in lifts and step sensors on escalators for customers like NS.
PHASE 04
End
Customer
The asset owners who see fewer shutdowns and extended step lifetimes.
NS
Launching · NL
Dutch national railway. The anchor launching customer where Stepguard has been piloted on station escalators.
Transport operators
Target · DE
Next market: Munich (MVG, 772+ escalators), Berlin (BVG), Frankfurt (FVG, 600+) and Deutsche Bahn. Entry through DHI subsidy and direct-to-operator sales.
Airports & malls
Expansion
Germany's 32 commercial airports plus ECE-group shopping centres — heavy-use environments where wear-and-tear drives breakdowns.
